Conclusion & Practice


Navigating difficult conversations takes time and effort, but remember that tools like this are available to help you center your focus and, hopefully, navigate them a little better. Just as no two people are alike, neither are situations. It may be easy to try and handle similar situations in the same way, but you may lose sight of the person that you're helping to develop and grow. As you build your experience and toolkit, make sure to re-visit the basics and continue to identify the areas that you may struggle.


Now that you have the informatin on how to prepare, conduct, and follow-up with a difficult conversation, it is important to put it to use!

Finish the Prompt

This can be a sure fire way to know that you have an issue. While it is common to "vent", you may find yourself consistently complaining about this employee. This behavior is your soul telling you to "talk to the person about X" because complaining is a temporary solution to actually addressing the problem.

Practice Makes Perfect

This worksheet contains a scripted conversation. This will give you a chance to say the lines out loud while focusing on the delivery. This can help you identify areas for improvement and to empathize with the employee by trying to bring the lines to life. You can choose to do this activity individually or with a partner.
